
# Video Game Sales Trends: 7th Generation Console Market Analysis

## Project Overview
This project provides a quantitative analysis of the global video game landscape, focusing specifically on the 7th generation console market (Nintendo Wii, Xbox 360, PlayStation 3). Developed for the INST762 Visual Analytics course, this dashboard and corresponding presentation apply established business strategy frameworks to historical sales data to explain market dominance, platform ecosystem value, and industry-wide digital transformations.

## Data Scope & Scale
The underlying dataset represents a massive collection of temporal gaming data:
* **Total Volume:** 8.92 Billion global software sales (units)
* **Game Titles:** 16,600 individual games
* **Time Span:** 35 years of temporal data
* **Platforms:** 31 unique console platforms

## Architecture & Technologies
* **Business Intelligence & Visualization:** Power BI (`.pbix`)
* **Presentation & Reporting:** PDF Executive Summary
* **Concepts Applied:** Visual Analytics, Quantitative Data Storytelling, Temporal Trend Analysis.

## Core Strategic Insights
The analysis is grounded in three academic frameworks to explain historical market movements:

### 1. Blue Ocean Strategy (Nintendo)
Nintendo established absolute market leadership (39.9% publisher market share) by pivoting away from the "Red Ocean" of raw processing competition. By introducing motion controls, Nintendo targeted casual families and non-gamers, unlocking a massive, uncontested consumer segment and winning the hardware units war.

### 2. Network Effects & Ecosystem Tipping (Microsoft & Sony)
Despite the Wii's hardware dominance, the Xbox 360 (979.9M software units) and PlayStation 3 (939.4M software units) demonstrated superior software attach rates. The data shows that the Xbox 360 reached critical mass early, sustaining a higher attach rate for over 50 months due to strong indirect network effects and third-party development incentives.

### 3. The Digital Transformation
Temporal analysis reveals a definitive industry shift from physical retail to digital distribution. Global physical sales peaked in 2008-2009 at 678 million units before facing a sharp "cliff." This decline correlates perfectly with the concurrent rise of digital storefronts like the Apple App Store and Steam.
